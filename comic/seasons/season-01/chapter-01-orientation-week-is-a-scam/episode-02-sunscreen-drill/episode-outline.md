# Episode 2 - Sunscreen Drill

## Type
Campus training comedy / orientation challenge

## Main characters
- Muci
- Padaruna
- Nia
- Snacri
- Coach Ray

## Episode promise
What Muci assumes will be a normal sunscreen lecture turns into an overproduced field exercise where every freshman reveals how differently they respond to rules, structure, and skincare discipline.

## Core plot
All first-years are marched to Sunscreen Field for what sounds like a simple educational session. Instead, Coach Ray treats sun protection like a competitive sport. The students are forced through cloud-cover simulations, reapplication drills, and rapid-fire trick questions about everyday exposure.

Padaruna is delighted and treats the entire event like a variety show challenge. Nia locks in with terrifying seriousness and starts performing like this is a final exam. Muci is simply trying to survive the absurdity, while Snacri quietly notices that the training handbook itself contains contradictions between different pages.

The scene is funny on the surface, but it also starts teaching the reader how the main cast thinks: Padaruna rides momentum, Nia trusts systems, Snacri trusts observation, and Muci becomes the person noticing that everyone around him is somehow treating madness as normal.

## Skincare knowledge
- Sunscreen habits matter even when the weather looks mild.
- Prevention works best when it becomes a routine rather than a panic response.
- Day-to-day awareness matters more than dramatic one-time correction.

## American humor beats
- Coach Ray behaves like clouds are an academic enemy.
- Padaruna respects the chaos as long as it has energy.
- Snacri's dry observation cuts through the theatrics.

## Foreshadowing
- Snacri notices that different students received different versions of the handbook.
- Version differences will become the first real investigative thread.

## Ending hook
Snacri hands Muci her manual and quietly tells him his copy is older.
