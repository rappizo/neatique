# Episode 8 - The Student Store Problem

## Type
Retail comedy / clue episode / school-history drift

## Main characters
- Muci
- Padaruna
- Snacri
- Tuck Trinket
- Artrans (briefly)

## Episode promise
An apparently silly trip to the campus store reveals that even merchandise history is being managed, and that old school language keeps resurfacing in places it should not.

## Core plot
Padaruna drags the others to the Student Store for school-exclusive essentials. The place feels like a luxury fairytale boutique with founder-themed goods, polished displays, and emotionally persuasive campus branding. Tuck Trinket, the store clerk, casually knows far too much about old product lines and discontinued founder materials.

Among the merchandise, the group finds old bookmarks carrying the previous school slogan: “Glow begins with observation.” Tuck remarks that these should have been discontinued, yet somehow showed back up in the inventory system.

Artrans spots the bookmark and goes unnervingly quiet. Muci presses him for answers, but Artrans retreats again instead of explaining.

## Skincare knowledge
- Packaging and hype are not the same as need.
- Scarcity marketing can distort judgment.
- Buying from anxiety is different from buying for use.

## American humor beats
- Padaruna falls into the classic trap of claiming she is not influenced while being completely influenced.
- Tuck delivers suspicious information with perfect customer-service cheer.

## Foreshadowing
- Old products are reappearing on purpose or through interference.
- A small ring-shaped emblem appears on the bookmark edge.

## Ending hook
The mysterious ring mark appears again, but nobody knows yet what it signifies.
