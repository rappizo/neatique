# Episode 5 - Hydration Greenhouse

## Type
Gentle knowledge episode / emotional slowdown / campus wonder

## Main characters
- Muci
- Padarana
- Snacri
- Professor Cera Lin (briefly)

## Episode promise
Inside the dreamy Hydration Greenhouse, Muci finally gets breathing room and begins learning the difference between hydration and moisture through Padarana's calm, specific way of caring.

## Core plot
After the pressure of orientation and class, Muci is assigned to a slower campus tour task inside the Hydration Greenhouse with Padarana. The space feels magical: curved glass, soft vapor, rounded instruments, and installations that make skincare education feel almost whimsical.

Muci casually mixes up hydration with moisturizing, and Padarana gently corrects him without making him feel stupid. Snacri adds one small but important observation: skin usually does not ask for drama, only attention and accuracy.

This is the first episode that lets the campus feel emotionally safe instead of merely funny. It also plants a bigger thematic question when Muci notices that an older campus plaque uses a different school motto than the current institution does.

## Skincare knowledge
- Hydration and moisturizing are related but not identical.
- Water content and moisture retention are different needs.
- Good skincare language needs to be more specific than “my skin is bad” or “my skin is dry.”

## American humor beats
- Muci is offended by how much more organized the greenhouse ecosystem is than his own routine.
- The greenhouse is so idealized it feels mildly competitive.

## Foreshadowing
- An old plaque says, “Glow begins with observation.”
- The current school branding says, “Glow starts with discipline.”
- The shift in language hints at a deeper ideological rewrite.

## Ending hook
Muci realizes for the first time that the school's past and present are not telling the same story.
