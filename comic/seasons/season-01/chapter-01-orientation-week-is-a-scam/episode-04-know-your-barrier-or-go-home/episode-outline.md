# Episode 4 - Know Your Barrier or Go Home

## Type
First-class episode / academic comedy / cast expansion

## Main characters
- Muci
- Nia
- Padaruna
- Snacri
- Artrans
- Professor Cera Lin

## Episode promise
The first real class introduces the school's skincare logic, reveals Nia as a top-tier student, and lets Artrans enter the story as someone far more sharp and watchful than he first appears.

## Core plot
Muci walks into a class titled “Know Your Barrier or Go Home” and immediately feels underqualified. Professor Cera Lin is composed, precise, and slightly terrifying. She uses examples of over-care, over-exfoliation, product hopping, and instant-results culture to make one thing clear: chaos disguised as effort still damages skin.

Nia dominates the room with near-perfect answers. Padaruna is eager but too momentum-driven. Muci is mostly trying not to get noticed. In the back row sits Artrans, who looks half asleep until Professor Cera Lin calls on him. His response is effortless, sarcastic, and exactly right.

The real shift comes when Artrans notices Muci's handbook and reacts. For the first time, something from the earlier comedy episodes gets acknowledged as potentially serious.

## Skincare knowledge
- Barrier stability is the foundation, not the glamorous extra.
- Intensity is not the same as effectiveness.
- Routines that constantly require damage control are already failing.

## American humor beats
- The course title itself sounds like a threat.
- Artrans describes a bad routine like it is a workplace incident report.
- Muci spends the entire class in survival mode.

## Foreshadowing
- Artrans clearly recognizes the older handbook version.
- He knows enough about school history to immediately classify it as wrong or forbidden.

## Ending hook
On the way out, Artrans tells Muci that the handbook should not still exist.
