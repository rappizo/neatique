## Episode promise
Inside the hidden Floor 4 archive, the group discovers Personal Glow Logs and realizes the old school used skincare observation not as ranking, but as a compassionate practice of long-term listening.

## Core plot
The hidden zone behind Floor 4 turns out not to be a horror chamber but a forgotten learning space. Rows of old drawers and storage cabinets contain Personal Glow Logs written by former students. These are not grades. They are records of skin behavior, sleep, stress, lifestyle, reactions, and gradual understanding.

Muci is moved by how humane the documents feel. Nia is fascinated because the logs are structured but not punitive. Padaruna is amazed that people were essentially journaling their pores. Snacri gives the cleanest interpretation: the old students were not rating themselves, they were listening.

The group then finds an empty drawer labeled for thesis-related cases, proving the archive once connected directly to The Glow Thesis.

## Skincare knowledge
- Skin diaries help patterns emerge over time.
- Observation is more useful than self-comparison.
- Data can support understanding without becoming a score.

## American humor beats

## Foreshadowing
