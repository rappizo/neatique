# Episode 4 - The Campus Store Drop

## Type
Trend satire / shopping episode / subtle threat

## Main characters
- Muci
- Padaruna
- Snacri
- Tuck Trinket
- Vela Sheen

## Episode promise
A shiny limited drop at the campus store becomes the chapter's clearest critique of fast-result culture, while also quietly tying Vela and the ring insignia closer to the larger conspiracy.

## Core plot
The Student Store launches a limited collection branded around the promise “Three Days to a Better Glow Routine.” Padaruna is instantly tempted and frames it as cultural participation rather than influence. Muci is intrigued but cautious. Snacri is openly suspicious of any timeline that sounds this neat.

Vela Sheen enters the scene with warmth, elegance, and just enough persuasive language to make the campaign feel almost reasonable. She suggests that first-years sometimes need structured guidance. The statement is not obviously wrong, which is exactly what makes it unnerving.

Later, Snacri notices that the campaign material carries the same small ring mark seen in older objects.

## Skincare knowledge
- Limited drops and trend framing can create false urgency.
- Faster is not the same as better.
- Needs should lead purchases, not anxiety or social pressure.

## American humor beats
- Padaruna insists she is not being influenced while embodying influence in real time.
- Tuck sells campus mythology like premium inventory.

## Foreshadowing
- The “three day” promise previews later fast-results ideology.
- The ring insignia now appears in contemporary promotional material.

## Ending hook
Snacri finds the ring mark printed on the store campaign material.
