# Episode 2 - Clouds Are Not SPF

## Type
Training comedy / sunscreen callback / character pressure

## Main characters
- Muci
- Padaruna
- Nia
- Coach Ray
- Sunny Spritz

## Episode promise
An overcast day sends the freshmen back to Sunscreen Field, where Coach Ray's dramatic teaching style turns prevention habits into campus comedy while also exposing how easily good habits can become anxiety.

## Core plot
The second field session happens under gray skies, which convinces many students that the day should be easier. Coach Ray responds like a betrayed general and pushes everyone through fresh drills built around the dangerous lie that clouds equal immunity.

Nia handles the rules flawlessly, but her approach is starting to look less calm and more rigid. Muci feels like he cannot do anything correctly enough. Padaruna asks chaotic but valid questions that accidentally make the lesson more useful.

The episode widens the gap between healthy structure and fear-based overperformance.

## Skincare knowledge
- Cloud cover does not eliminate the need for everyday protection.
- A steady habit beats emergency correction.
- Prevention should support calm routine, not create panic.

## American humor beats
- Coach Ray treats weather denial like a moral failing.
- Padaruna's questions sound unserious but are actually practical.

## Foreshadowing
- Vela Sheen appears at the edge of the training field observing students.
- She notices Nia specifically.

## Ending hook
Vela quietly records something after watching Nia perform.
