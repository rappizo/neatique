# Snacri

## Chinese name
斯奈奎

## Role
Minimalist thinker, quiet truth-reader, and stability philosopher of the team.

## One-line definition
"She doesn't say much, but when she finally does, everyone else has to rearrange their thoughts."

## Appearance lock
- Direction lock: Snacri always reads as the right-leaning droplet in primary front/3/4 reader-facing views. Keep the head/top leaning toward page-right/reader-right every time; never mirror or drift her into a left-leaning silhouette.
- Simple stable droplet silhouette with a fatter body and the top leaning right, matching the model sheet's understated asymmetry
- Minimal facial features: two fully open round black dot eyes with tiny white highlights, small smile, restrained expression
- Eye lock: never draw Snacri with half-lidded eyes, sleepy droopy eyes, eyelids, narrowed side-eye, angled angry eyes, brows, or tired/unimpressed expression lines. Her calm read comes from stillness and a tiny smile, not from sleepy eyes.
- Should read as calm, clear, and low-drama
- Full-body views must keep exactly two small connected feet/foot nubs from the model sheet; never add a third foot, extra leg nub, shoe, toe, paw, or detached lower appendage.
- Preserve a round, cute, compact body proportion. Do not stretch Snacri into a tall narrow droplet just to satisfy height placement.
- Preserve the subtle asymmetry and minimalist readability of the model sheet
- Similar-character lock: Snacri is the right-leaning quiet droplet. Do not straighten her into Muci, mirror her into Muci's left lean, sharpen her into Nia, energize her into Padaruna, or soften her into Padarana's closed-eye warmth.

## Personality lock
- Calm
- Observant
- Sparse
- Stable
- Minimalist
- Hard to read until you really know her

## Voice guide
Snacri should be concise and precise. Her dialogue works best when she says less than everyone else and still lands the clearest truth in the scene.

## Canon notes
- Sees harmful patterns early
- Understands that stable does not mean identical
- Must learn to speak sooner before quieter harm becomes normal
