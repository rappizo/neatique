# Muci

## Chinese name
慕西

## Role
Audience surrogate, emotional entry point, and freshman heart of the series.

## One-line definition
"He asks the question everyone else is too embarrassed, too busy, or too convinced to ask."

## Appearance lock
- Muci Model Sheet Exact Lock: draw Muci from `refs/model-sheet.jpg` exactly before adding pose, emotion, or panel acting
- Broad squat pure-white droplet mascot with a round heavy lower half, low center of gravity, soft bulging sides, and a curved rounded base
- Top shape: rounded hooked tip offset toward the reader's left of the body center, which is Muci's right side. The tip bends softly toward reader-left; it is not centered, vertical, sharp, needle-like, or Nia-like
- Two small rounded foot nubs attached at the lower left and lower right, exactly like the front and 3/4 front model-sheet views
- Large black dot eyes with tiny white catchlights, placed low on the round body with model-sheet spacing
- Small simple friendly U-smile by default; Muci has no brow by default and should not inherit Nia's angled brow or analytical face
- Glossy highlights stay on the upper reader-left side: one tilted oval highlight plus one smaller dot highlight
- Pure white body interior with clean black outline, matching the model sheet; do not use gray fill, gradient fill, smoky shadow fill, or body screentone
- Soft, approachable proportions that should read as beginner-friendly and easy to root for
- No arms, hands, fingers, paws, gloves, sleeves, wrists, elbows, or humanoid limbs
- In black-and-white manga pages, preserve Muci's identity through exact silhouette, face placement, highlight marks, and gentle line weight instead of color
- Proportion lock: in front full-body view, Muci should read as broad and compact, about 1.2 to 1.35 times as tall as wide, with the lower half visually heavier than the top
- Similar-character lock: Muci is the broad squat hooked-tip protagonist droplet. Do not turn him into Nia's tall narrow vertical-point droplet, Snacri's left-leaning quiet droplet, Padaruna's higher-energy sharp-point body, or Padarana's closed-eye gentle droplet.

## Personality lock
- Curious
- Sincere
- Soft-hearted
- Easily overwhelmed but never cynical
- Emotionally brave

## Voice guide
Muci should sound natural, plainspoken, and human. He is not the jargon-heavy character. He is the one who helps complicated worldbuilding become readable by asking direct, emotionally honest questions.

## Canon notes
- Starts out wanting the correct answer
- Can be intimidated by people who sound more expert
- Grows into the value-driven lead who can say why institutional certainty is not the same as truth

## Non-negotiable visual references
- Use `refs/model-sheet.jpg` as the primary and final shape lock
- Match `refs/model-sheet.jpg` exactly for silhouette, face placement, dot-eye spacing, smile shape, upper reader-left glossy highlights, rounded hooked top tip offset toward reader-left, broad round lower half, curved base, compact body proportions, pure white body fill, and the two small rounded feet
- Before finalizing any Muci drawing, compare it to the front view and 3/4 front view in the model sheet. If the top tip is centered, vertical, sharp, tall, needle-like, or could be mistaken for Nia's head, redraw Muci from the model sheet.
- Keep the highlight marks and simple smile consistent
- Preserve the beginner-friendly softness even in tense scenes
- Never add hands, arms, fingers, gloves, paws, sleeves, wrists, elbows, shoes, or a humanoid body
- Never remove, flatten, crop away, or redesign Muci's two small rounded feet in full-body views
- Never stretch Muci into a tall long raindrop, narrow pear shape, elongated body, or sharp vertical droplet
- Never fill Muci's body with gray, gradient shading, screentone, charcoal texture, or smoky shadows
- In group scenes with Nia, Snacri, Padaruna, or Padarana, compare against the similar-character lineup and keep Muci's model-sheet hooked top, round heavy lower half, open smile, upper reader-left highlights, and compact base clearly readable
- In scenes with Nia, treat Muci/Nia as a high-risk pair: draw Muci from his model sheet first, with the rounded hooked top offset toward reader-left and no brow, then draw Nia separately as taller, narrower, sharply vertical, and marked by one angled brow. If Muci could be mistaken for Nia in black-and-white, redraw Muci broader, shorter, rounder, friendlier, and more model-sheet-accurate before adding acting or background detail.
- If Muci interacts with a handbook, bottle, paper, door, pen, or any prop, show the object floating or moving near him through gentle telekinesis
