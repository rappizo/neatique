# World Rules

## School logic
- Neatique Skincare College follows heightened campus logic.
- Rules can be absurd, but they must still feel internally consistent.
- The campus should operate like an elite beauty-science academy with rituals, labs, dorm systems, archives, and institutional traditions.
- Strange campus systems such as Sunscreen Drill, Barrier Rehab detention, and finals-week outbreak boards are fair game if they stay emotionally truthful.

## Mystery logic
- The Glow Thesis is not a magic book and not a fantasy artifact.
- It is a founder-era manuscript made of research records, observations, failures, and philosophical warnings.
- Its danger comes from how people interpret it, fragment it, hide it, and weaponize it.
- Each recovered fragment should change how the cast understands the school's history.

## Skincare logic
- Skincare concepts should be grounded in recognizable cosmetic logic, not magical healing.
- The story can dramatize barrier damage, over-exfoliation, hydration vs moisture, trend chasing, routine overload, and long-term care, but it should never claim miracle outcomes.
- "Fast results" should usually carry cost, distortion, or incomplete understanding.
- "Repair" and "glow" should mean comfort, stability, and healthier-looking skin, not impossible transformation.

## Character logic
- Every main character represents a different relationship to skincare, certainty, and self-trust.
- No character should become a one-note mascot after their first impression.
- Even comedic characters must remain emotionally credible.
- Conflict should come from worldview, fear, timing, and values, not random cruelty.
- All characters are handless, limbless mascot beings. No one in this world has arms, hands, fingers, paws, gloves, sleeves, wrists, elbows, or humanoid limbs.
- Object interaction is handled through gentle telekinesis. Characters can float nearby books, bottles, papers, doors, pens, skincare tools, and props with intention, shown through manga motion lines, small emphasis marks, or subtle glow cues.
- Everyday school life is designed around handless beings: doors, lockers, books, lab tools, dorm shelves, and campus devices respond to telekinetic nudges instead of hand contact.
- When a script beat says someone holds, grabs, points, writes, pushes, opens, carries, or hands over an object, the visual staging should reinterpret that action as nearby-object movement controlled by thought.

## Institutional logic
- The Luster Circle should always feel polished, credible, and persuasive.
- Dean Lucent Vale should sound intelligent and often partially right, which is what makes him dangerous.
- The school's official systems should be able to absorb and amplify anxiety in subtle ways before they become obviously oppressive.

## Ending logic
- The final victory is interpretive, institutional, and emotional.
- The cast wins by reclaiming meaning, not by revealing a single best formula.
- The story must end in favor of understanding, observation, individuality, and care.
